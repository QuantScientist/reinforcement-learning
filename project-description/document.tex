\subsection*{Trials}
A series of trials is a user's page visits on the site during a single session.

\subsection*{Agent = advertisement system}
The agent is the advertisement system that decides which ad to show to the
user.

\subsection*{Actions = showing advertisements}
After each trial the agent, or the advertisement system, may show a single
advertisement to the user. The reward of this action is modeled by a
probability density function conditioned on "interest keywords". What I mean
with this, is that if the advertisement's target interests align with those of
the user's interest, the probability of receiving a high reward is high. If
there are really no aligning interests, then zero reward is given.

In the worst case, showing a mismatching ad can result in the user prematurely
leaving the site, signaled by a negative reward.

This reward can be thought of as whether or not the user clicks on the ad, and
given that he/she does, how much "time/money/interest" does she spend on that
"site".
